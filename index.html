About Maai

Sindhutai Sapkal (14 November 1948 - 4 January 2022) was an Indian social worker who devoted her entire life to raising orphaned children. Born in Wardha, Maharashtra, she overcame immense personal hardship to become a mother to thousands.

She was married at 12 to a man 20 years older and was abandoned during pregnancy. She gave birth in a cow shelter and survived by begging. Despite these hardships, she adopted and raised over 1,500 orphans. She gave up her biological daughter so she could treat all children equally.

She built homes, gave speeches to raise funds, and founded institutions like “Sanmati Bal Niketan.” Her love earned her the name “Mai” (Mother). Her life story inspired many across the world.

She was honored with over 750 awards, including the Padma Shri in 2021. Her message: “I will never take anything from anyone, but I will not let anyone go hungry.”
